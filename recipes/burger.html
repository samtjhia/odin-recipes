Burger Recipe

Home

An image of a Burger.

To make a classic burger at home, start with the patties: mix 1 pound of ground beef (80% lean) with 1 teaspoon of salt, ½ teaspoon of black pepper, and any additional seasonings you like (such as garlic powder or onion powder). Form the mixture into 4 equal patties, making a slight indentation in the center of each to prevent them from puffing up while cooking. Preheat your grill or a skillet over medium-high heat. Cook the patties for about 4-5 minutes on each side for medium doneness, or until they reach your desired level of doneness. If you like cheese, add a slice of your favorite cheese (like cheddar or American) on top of each patty during the last minute of cooking to let it melt.

While the patties are cooking, prepare your burger buns by toasting them lightly on the grill or in a toaster. Assemble your burgers by placing a cooked patty on the bottom half of each bun. Top with your favorite condiments such as ketchup, mustard, mayonnaise, or barbecue sauce. Add fresh toppings like lettuce, tomato slices, pickles, and sliced onions. You can also include extras like crispy bacon or avocado slices for added flavor. Place the top half of the bun on each burger and serve immediately with a side of fries or a salad. Enjoy your homemade burgers with friends and family!

Ingredients

  • 1 pound ground beef (80% lean)
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • ½ teaspoon black pepper
  • Optional seasonings (garlic powder, onion powder)
  • 4 burger buns
  • 4 slices of cheese (cheddar, American, etc.)
  • Condiments (ketchup, mustard, mayonnaise, barbecue sauce)
  • Fresh toppings (lettuce, tomato slices, pickles, onions)
  • Optional extras (bacon, avocado slices)

Steps

  1. Mix ground beef with salt, pepper, and optional seasonings.
  2. Form the mixture into 4 equal patties with an indentation in the center to prevent puffing.
  3. Preheat the grill or skillet over medium-high heat.
  4. Cook the patties for 4-5 minutes on each side for medium doneness.
  5. Add a slice of cheese on top of each patty during the last minute of cooking to melt.
  6. Toast the burger buns lightly on the grill or in a toaster.
  7. Assemble the burgers by placing a cooked patty on the bottom half of each bun.
  8. Top with condiments and fresh toppings like lettuce, tomato, pickles, and onions.
  9. Include extras like bacon or avocado if desired.
  10. Place the top half of the bun on each burger.
  11. Serve immediately with a side of fries or a salad.
